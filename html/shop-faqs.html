Frequently Asked Questions

1. Should I buy organic vegetables?

Organic foods are generally good for you and for the environment. Even though organic fruits and vegetables are more expensive than conventional product, the true long term benefits to health and environment are suggested to be sizeable.

2. How do you identify organic vegetables?

Organic vegetables look similar to conventional vegetables. Since there is no use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, they may often look natural, smaller, irregular and less attractive. A dot, scar or hole is not uncommon. Identify by trusted source and not the product.

3. Which vegetables and fruits are better organic?

Prioritize organic fruits and vegetables which typically have a high use of fertilizers and pesticides or are grown using unhygienic inputs in farms. The following come on top in India. 1. Leafy vegetables 2. Chilli 3. Brinjal 4. Bhendi 5. Gourds 6. Banana 7. Beans 8. Capsicum 9. Cauliflower 10. Cabbage 11. Groundnut 12. Rice

4.Is organic food safer?

Organic farming does not employ chemical fertilizers & pesticides, does not utilize sewage water and hence has lower pesticide residues and contamination in food. This translates to safer food. Natural and biological control agents are considered to be less toxic and to leave less or no residues, though this is to be proven conclusively.

5.How do you know organic food is really organic?

The number one way to tell organic food is by its source – procure from a reliable professional farmer organization that is knowledgeable and trustworthy. Organic food is likely to be less uniform in appearance and may have a scar or a dot. A few insects or worms at times are to be expected. Organic food may not store well for long due to non use of storage chemicals, wax & fungicide coatings. Many times, organic food tastes better due to balanced nutrition of plants.

6. Why Is Organic Food More Expensive?

Producing organic food and maintaining their organic status usually costs more for farmers, which can lead to organic food being slightly more expensive. Because of the fewer amount of pesticides used, organic farming requires more intensive labor and maintenance. Another reason is that organic producers often don’t use preservatives, which is more natural but leads to shorter shelf life.

7. How Do You Properly Wash Organic Food to Remove Pesticides?

The Food and Drug Administration department of America recommends washing organic produce with regular water and drying it afterward with a towel or a cloth to reduce bacteria and toxic substances. The FDA advises consumers to also wash food with tough skin, like cucumbers and avocados, to ensure that bacteria won’t transfer to the food by a knife. Scrubbing vegetables and fruits with a brush is also recommended.
